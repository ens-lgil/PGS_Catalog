Ancestry Categories

Our Ancestry categories are based on the publication Morales, J. et al. Genome Biol. (2018). (Table 1).

Ancestry Distribution

The ancestry distribution provides a summary information about the ancestry of the individuals used to develop and evaluate each polygenic score. The distribution is calculated and reported for each stage of the Polygenic Score construction (variants association, development/training and evaluation).
The distribution calculation for each stydy stage is described in the following table:

Study stageDistribution calculationExample
Source of Variant Associations
(GWAS)		 Count individuals ancestry for each associated sample and then group them per ancestry category.
The distribution is calculated by dividing each individuals ancestry category by the total number of individuals.
  • Sample A: 1500 individuals - European
  • Sample B: 2500 individuals - European
  • Sample C: 1000 individuals - East Asian
Distribution:
European: 80% (4000 individuals out of 5000 individuals)
East Asian: 20% (1000 individuals out of 5000 individuals)
Score Development Same distribution calculation as Source of Variant Associations (GWAS)
Score Evaluation

Assign one ancestry category per Sample Set. When a Sample Set contains more than one sample, the sample ancestries are combined.
The distribution is calculated by dividing the number of Sample Sets with a same ancestry category by the total number of Sample Sets.

Ancestry Filters

Study stages

First of all, you need to select on which PGS stage you want to filter the ancestries. Here are the different choices from the dropdown "Samples included in":

Development [V, D] Corresponds to the combination of the stages "Source of Variant Associations (GWAS)" and "Score Development"
GWAS [V] Corresponds to the stage "Source of Variant Associations (GWAS)".
Score Development [D] Corresponds to the stage "Score Development".
PGS Evaluation [E] Corresponds to the stage "Score Evaluation".
All Stages combined [V, D, E] Corresponds to the combination of the stages "Source of Variant Associations (GWAS)", "Score Development" and "Score Evaluation".
Ancestry selection

Then you can filter the ancestries.

Note: You can combine the different filters listed below.
Multiple-Ancestries Filter PGS that include data from multiple ancestry groups at the selected study stage.
Excluding Europeans Filter PGS that do not include data from European ancestry individuals at the selected study stage.
Ancestry selection
("Select an ancestry" dropdown)		 Filter PGS that include data of individuals from the selected ancestry at the selected study stage. This also filter the ancestries within the Multi-ancestry distributions.
